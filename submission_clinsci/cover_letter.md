We submit the manuscript entitled **"PLIN2+/TREM1+ Macrophages Are a Non-Redundant Inflammatory Signaling Hub in Human Atherosclerosis across Three Vascular Beds"** for consideration for publication in *Clinical Science*.

Our study re-analyzes the largest publicly available human atherosclerotic plaque single-cell atlas (259,116 cells; 73 donors; 11 datasets) spanning carotid, coronary, and femoral arteries. Through ligand-receptor network modeling, in-silico TREM1 perturbation, independent external validation (GSE131778, coronary artery), and analytical reproducibility testing (GSE155512, carotid artery), we identify PLIN2+/TREM1+ macrophages as a conserved, non-redundant inflammatory signaling hub. Virtual TREM1 ablation selectively dampens inflammatory communication without triggering compensatory pathway activation—a pharmacologically favorable profile for chronic cardiovascular disease. A femoral-specific FAO-SIRT1-MHC-II metabolic-epigenetic axis is mechanistically independent of the conserved hub architecture.

This work is directly aligned with *Clinical Science*'s mission of "translating molecular bioscience and experimental research into medical insights," spanning the journal's core areas of cardiovascular system, inflammation, and vascular biology. The findings carry direct translational relevance: TREM1 blockade is predicted to achieve anti-inflammatory efficacy without immunosuppression, a critical consideration for long-term treatment of chronic atherosclerosis.
